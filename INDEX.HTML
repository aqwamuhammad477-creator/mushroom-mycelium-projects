Mushroom Mycelium Growth Analysis

Application of Derivatives in Malaysian Agrotech (Grey Oyster Mushroom)

The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysia, mushroom farmers use rubberwood sawdust as a substrate. We need to track the radial growth (mm) of mycelium over time (days).

The derivative f'(x) represents the instantaneous growth rate. Knowing this helps agrotechnologists detect if a batch is lagging due to high humidity or contamination.

The Mathematical Model

Logistic Growth Function:

f(x) = L / (1 + e-k(x-x₀))

First Derivative (Growth Rate):

f'(x) = k · f(x) · (1 - f(x)/L)
  • L (80mm): Carrying capacity (Petri dish size)
  • k (0.55): Growth steepness (Vigor)
  • x₀ (Day 7): Midpoint of colonization
